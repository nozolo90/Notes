====== Liturgical Year ======
Created Wednesday 21 March 2018

The Liturgical year is like the church's seasons.

=== Colors used ===
//During each season on the liturgical year, clergy's vestments and church curtains are decorated with a certain color.//
* **Purple** is used for Advent and Lent** ->** The color represents penance and fasting
* **Red** during Pentecost** ->** the color stands for goodness and strength
* **Green** is used for Ordinary Time **->** the color stands for growth and hope
* **White/Gold** is used for Christmas and Easter**: ->**the colors represent light and joy

=== Advent ===
* Advent is the waiting time for the coming of baby Jesus.
* The season begins 4 Sundays before Christmas, and ends on Christmas eve.
**During Lent**, **Christians prepare for the coming of Jesus** at Christmas by:
	* Thinking about their lives
	* Praying and attending mass.
	* Doing confession.
	* Preforming good deeds.

=== Advent wreath ===
The advent wreath is a religious symbol.
* The circle represents God's never ending love which both have no beginning and no end.
* The candles all represent Jesus, the light of the world.
**Colors of the Candles on the wreath**
* //Purple Candles - //these candles represent the **need to prepare** for Jesus' coming, and are the first three candles to be lit.
* //Pink Candle// - these candles represent the **joy of being close to Christmas**, and are lit one week before the white candle.
* //White Candle// - this candle represents the **birth of Jesus**, and is lit on Christmas eve at the easter vigil.

=== Details - Christmas ===
* Christmas comes from "the mass of Christ's birth", later shortened to "Christ's mass" and now "Christmas"
__The Incarnation__
* The **Incarnation** is the belief that God became human in Jesus Christ.
* Christians believe that this is a sign of God's great love for the world, that he sent down his only son for us.
__The Epiphany__
* The epiphany is the celebration of the arrival of the 3 wise men aka **the magi**.
* It is celebrated on the 6th of January
* It made Christians aware that Jesus is the God for all.

Ends on Sunday after epiphany.

=== Lent ===
* During the original "Lent" Jesus spent 40 days and 40 nights in the desert
* Christians try to focus on Jesus and his message.
* Lent is a time to develop a closer relationship with God.
* During Lent, Christians fast, pray and do good deeds.
* Lent begins on the day after **Shrove/Pancake Tuesday**, Ash Wednesday.
* On ash Wednesday, Christians go to mass to get ashes placed on their forehead. This is sign that they are sorry and will try change.

=== Holy Week ===
Holy week is the last week of Lent. Here are the special days.
* On **Palm Sunday: **Jesus entered Jerusalem. He was welcomed with people placing palms on the ground for his donkey. It is the **beginning of the Easter triduum**.
* On **Holy Thursday: **Jesus had the last supper with his squad.
* On **Good Friday: **Jesus is carried the cross up Calvary/Golgotha and was crucified on Golgotha
* On **Holy Saturday: **Jesus lied in the tomb. The Easter vigil takes place.
* On **Easter Sunday: **Jesus rises from the dead, appearing first to Mary.
